# Canon Lore: Royal Arms, Astrals, and Royal Line

## Analysis

### Royal Arms
Royal Arms encode Lucian political theology into material form: each weapon is both inherited authority and accumulated burden. They are dispersed across tombs, requiring pilgrimage-like retrieval rather than passive inheritance. This design links kingship to trial, memory, and obligation. Canon framing implies that wielding these arms is not about loot acquisition but about becoming legible to the lineage’s mandate.

### The Six (Hexatheon)
The Six are not constant interventionists; they are sovereign metaphysical actors whose engagement is conditional, ritualized, and frequently catastrophic at human scale. Their relevance to the plot is mediated through covenant structures, messengers, and the Oracle’s office. This keeps divine action politically consequential: each Astral interaction reorders military and civic reality, not just character power levels.

### Oracle and covenant system
The Oracle institution is structurally indispensable because it mediates communication between human polities and divine actors. Covenant/revelation events are framed as tests and negotiations with severe costs, making the Oracle role operationally dangerous and politically central. In canon terms, this is a duty architecture, not a symbolic priesthood.

### Messengers
Messengers formalize divine-human communication as a networked system rather than sporadic miracles. Their finite number and intermediary role reinforce that access to divine will is structured, interpreted, and institutionally channeled. This limits arbitrary claims of divine sanction by mortal factions.

### Lucian royal line
The Lucian line is defined less by heredity alone than by custodianship obligations tied to the Crystal, Ring, and the True King framework. Canon sources repeatedly link sovereignty to sacrificial cost, worthiness, and end-state duty. As a result, succession is portrayed as a narrowing path of responsibility culminating in existential payment rather than dynastic privilege.
