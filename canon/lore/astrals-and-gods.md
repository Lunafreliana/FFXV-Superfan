# Canon Lore: Astrals (The Six)
## Analysis
### Overview: The Six / Hexatheon
The Astrals are a structured pantheon, not a loose myth list. Canon describes them as watchers over the world since ancient times and ties human-divine mediation to the Oracle and Messengers. In narrative function, Astrals are civilization-scale forces whose interventions reconfigure politics, warfare, and survival conditions.
### Titan (the Archaean)
Titan is framed as earth-linked power and one of the central covenant figures in the overland arc. The Archaean encounter marks a major escalation point from regional conflict to explicit god-trial progression.
### Ramuh (the Fulgurian)
Ramuh is represented as a lightning-aligned Astral whose favor is formalized through the Mark of the Fulgurian, with explicit gameplay/lore messaging that he supports the struggling king.
### Shiva (the Glacian)
Shiva is ice-aligned and narratively complex: she is both one of the Six protecting the world and, in chronology/research records, a target of imperial anti-Astral warfare in the Ghorovas Rift.
### Leviathan (the Hydraean)
Leviathan is sea-aligned and tied to Altissia’s covenant crisis axis. The Hydraean encounter is one of the key points where personal trajectory and world-scale stakes fuse.
### Bahamut (the Draconian)
Bahamut is explicitly tied to Oracle origins in Cosmogony lines (first Oracle, trident, stars-gift framework), making the Draconian central to the institutional architecture of king/oracle duty.
### Ifrit (the Infernian)
Ifrit is repeatedly framed as hostile to humankind relative to other Astrals and linked to deep-age war/betrayal traditions. He serves as one of the most direct embodiments of antagonistic divine force in late arc material.
