# Canon Lore: Crystal, Kings, and Starscourge

## Analysis

### Crystal
The Crystal is the foundational power-object around which Lucian sovereignty, prophecy, and geopolitical conflict are organized. It is not merely an energy source; it is a legitimacy engine that determines who can enact kingly duty under the Lucii framework. In practical history, wars, treaties, and occupations become intelligible as attempts to capture or neutralize Crystal-mediated order.

### The Wall and royal burden
The Wall operationalizes Crystal power into territorial defense, but at direct physiological and lifespan cost to the reigning king. This converts abstract sovereignty into continual sacrifice. Canon descriptions of Regis’s condition frame the Wall as a strategic miracle purchased through personal depletion.

### Starscourge
Starscourge is the setting’s civilization-ending pressure system: ecological, temporal, and social. It expands darkness, increases daemon prevalence, and compresses habitable routines. The central plot is therefore not simply succession conflict but a race against planetary deterioration.

### Daemons and night
Daemons are the visible edge of Starscourge in everyday life. Their nocturnal dominance restructures transport, settlement patterns, and survival behavior. Night ceases to be a neutral temporal category and becomes a threat interval that can eventually consume the day/night social order.

### True King framework
The True King paradigm links Crystal legitimacy, royal lineage, and Starscourge resolution into a single end-state doctrine. Its narrative logic is sacrificial and terminal: the king’s role is to end the scourge at irreversible personal cost. This frames the finale as fulfillment of burdened duty, not heroic bonus outcome.
