# Canon Plot Points: Cross-Media
## Analysis
### Kingsglaive as collapse perspective
*Kingsglaive* provides the military-ground perspective on treaty-day collapse, clarifying operational details that the main game references from distance. Its key value is not contradiction but granularity: it enriches the fall of Insomnia, Regis’s final actions, and the Ring transfer chain.
### Brotherhood as pre-road social architecture
*Brotherhood* functions as interpersonal backfill for the core party, explaining why the road narrative’s trust dynamics operate immediately under stress. It does not replace game canon beats; it contextualizes reaction patterns and prior bonds.
### Comrades and world-state continuity
*Comrades* extends perspective into post-fall survival conditions and resistance continuity, making explicit that the collapse period is socially inhabited rather than a narrative blackout. It supports interpretation of Lucis’s aftermath as prolonged collective struggle.
### Episode overlays (Gladiolus / Prompto / Ignis / Ardyn)
The episode content adds focused viewpoint detail around gaps in the core route. Their strongest canon utility is motivational and situational resolution: why key separations occur, what happens during off-screen intervals, and how specific ideological conflicts intensify.
### Continuity handling guidance
For editorial consistency, treat cross-media content as layered elaboration around mainline anchors unless explicitly flagged otherwise. The safest method is: main-game chronology first, then cross-media inserts at documented hinge points.
