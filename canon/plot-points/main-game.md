# Canon Plot Points: Main Game
## Analysis
### Departure under treaty cover
The opening departure is structurally deceptive: publicly a marriage-route journey, strategically a survival contingency amid suspected imperial bad faith. This dual framing is essential for interpreting Regis’s choices and the party’s immediate displacement.
### Fall of Insomnia and state collapse
The treaty-day assault is the political rupture that converts a dynastic storyline into dispossession narrative. With Regis dead and Insomnia lost, Noctis’s arc shifts from heir-apparent to burdened claimant in exile-like conditions.
### Overland consolidation (Leide–Duscae–Cleigne)
The middle arc combines survival logistics, relationship stabilization, and ritual progression (Royal Arms / Astral encounters). This period is where Noctis’s role hardens from reluctant prince to active bearer of unavoidable duty.
### Altissia turning point
Altissia fuses personal and metaphysical stakes into irreversible escalation. After this point, the narrative’s cost profile steepens and return-to-normal scenarios collapse.
### Crystal entry, decade gap, World of Ruin
Noctis’s Crystal interval reframes the conflict from contested sovereignty to terminal world-restoration mandate. The ten-year absence externalizes the price of delayed fulfillment and broadens the story from party journey to species-level survival.
### Final Insomnia and sacrificial closure
The finale resolves the Ardyn conflict and the scourge crisis through sacrificial kingship logic. The ending is not merely tactical victory; it is doctrinal completion of the True King burden.
